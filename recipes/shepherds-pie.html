Shepherd's Pie

Description

Shepherd's pie is a savory dish of cooked minced meat topped with cheese mashed potatoes and baked. Traditionally the meat used is mutton, although lamb or even beef may be used instead. Along with the meat, a mix of vegetables may be included as well. These are typically onions, peas, carrots, and sometimes corn.

When I make this dish, I sometimes use ground turkey instead of red meat, and use whatever vegetables I happen to have on hand. I've added mushrooms, brocolli, and cauliflour and have always been satisfied with the final product.

Ingredients

Meat filling

  • 1 lb minced or ground lamb / beef (Or any meat that you prefer)
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 cup chopped yellow onion
  • 1/2 cup chopped carrots
  • 1/2 cup frozen peas
  • 1/2 cup frozen corn
  • 2 garlic cloves (minced)
  • 1 cup beef broth
  • 1 tbsp Worcestershire sauce
  • 2 tbsp tomato paste
  • 2 tbsp all purpose flour
  • 2 tsp dried parsley
  • 1 tsp dried rosemary
  • 1 tsp dried thyme
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp ground black pepper

Potato topping

  • 1 1/2 - 2 lb. russet potatoes (about 2 large potatoes peeled and cut into 1 inch cubes)
  • 8 tbsp unsalted butter (1 stick)
  • 1/3 cup half & half
  • 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1/4 tsp ground black pepper
  • 1/4 cup parmesan cheese

Steps

  1. Heat a large skillet at medium-high heat and add the oilwhen hot. Add the onions and cook for 5 minutes, stirring occasionally.
  2. Add the meat and break it apart. Add carrots, herbs, salt, and pepper, and stir well. Cook for 6-8 minutes, until the meat is browned, stirring occasionally.
  3. Add the Worcestershire sauce and the minced garlic. Stir and cook for 1 minute.
  4. Add the flour and tomato paste. Stir until no clumps remain.
  5. Add the broth, peas, and corn. Bring the liquid to a boil then reduce to a simmer and cook for 5 minutes. When done, set mixture aside and preheat the oven to 400 degrees F.
  6. For the mashed potato topping, place the potatoes in a large pot and cover with water. Bring the water to a boil. Reduce to a simmer and cook for 10-15 minutes, or until the potatoes are fork tender.
  7. Drain the potatoes and return them to the pot. Allow any remaining water to evaporate in hot pot.
  8. Add the butter, half & half, salt, and pepper. Mash the potatoes and stir everything together.
  9. Add the cheese and mix to conbine.
  10. Pour the meat mixture into a casserole or baking dish. Spread it out into an even layer. Cover with the mashed potatoes and smooth them over the meat carefully.
  11. Bake uncovered for 25-30 minutes. Let cool before serving.
